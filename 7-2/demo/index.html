Social media addiction is a matter of great concern，which has caused a lot of mental illness, such as bipolar disorder and depression. People constantly pursue the connection with everything through the smart devices in their hands. But they ignore the connection with themselves.
ZukunftIch (in English: Future Me) is an installation mirror, which can reflect the behavior of  the audience’s, but unlike normal mirror，in an abstract, deferred way. Through the installation, the audience can feel themselves from the perspective of time and space, and re-establish the connection with themselves.

Leon Diemen: “Where the water flows, it will emit an expanding and deepening trench. The next time water flows, the water flows naturally along the previous trench. The same is true of the brain, where the imprint of external things in the nervous system creates an increasingly appropriate path for itself.” 

The reason why social media is addictive to users is also that this addiction model is used. This method allows users to make behavioral addiction themselves. This behavioral addiction model, called “hook,” is mainly made up of four parts, namely triggers, behaviors, rewards, and investments.

  • Do you feel preoccupied with the Internet (think about previous online activity or anticipate next online session)?
  • Do you feel the need to use the Internet with increasing amounts of time in order to achieve satisfaction?
  • Have you repeatedly made unsuccessful efforts to control, cut back, or stop Internet use?
  • Do you feel restless, moody, depressed, or irritable when attempting to cut down or stop Internet use
  • Do you stay online longer than originally intended
  • Have you jeopardized or risked the loss of significant relationship, job, educational or career opportunity because of the Internet?
  • Have you lied to family members, therapist, or others to conceal the extent of involvement with the Internet?
  • Do you use the Internet as a way of escaping from problems or of relieving a dysphoric mood (e.g., feelings of helplessness, guilt, anxiety, depression)?

It turns out that in all addiction treatments, the most important thing is to stimulate people’s self-control and self-consciousness, whether through “mindfulness meditation”, biofeedback or equine therapy. These treatments draw people’s attention (consciousness) to their own thoughts and actions, which are often ignored in our daily lives.

Daumenlutscher is a very famous German fairy tale. It’s about a kid who loves to suck his thumb. His mother cut off his thumb to stop her from continuing the act.

Ironically, in many places electric shocks or physical punishment is still the way to cure the addition, which is a very sad thing. What we really have to do is evoke people’s sense of self.
